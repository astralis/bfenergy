Invest In Knowledge

To achieve our goals, we are invested in being knowledgeable advocates. While we invite you to benefit from the following sources, we are primarily investing in learning through living. Our focus is to engage with leaders in these fields and have meaningful dialogue.

Security Humanity Sustainability Public Opinion

Security: Energy and AI are Important Security Issues

  • Energy Resilience: American progress rests on a reliable and efficient energy grid. U.S. Energy Secretary Wright crucially notes that “nuclear fusion will soon power the world.” (BBC)
  • Technological Sovereignty: As China continues to invest billions in American companies and infrastructure, it is paramount that the U.S. works to create a secure, independent, and stable infrastructure. (CRS)
  • Advanced Energy Network: To win the AI race, America needs better and more reliable energy transmission. Read more.
  • AI & Energy for Society and Global Affairs: View the presentation slides from Brad Groux at the World Affairs Council, Houston. View Slides.

Humanity: Energy and AI are Human Rights Issues

  • Accessibility of Essential Services: Availability of clean water, accessibility of food, and disaster relief are all primarily energy problems. Nuclear fusion holds the promise of solving these problems. (CMU)
  • Resource Optimization: AI and Blockchain can optimize humanitarian campaigns. The UN World Food Program saved over $67 million in Ukraine by utilizing this strategy. (UN)

Sustainability: Energy and AI are Environmental Issues

  • Sustainable Energy: U.S. energy demands are set to increase by 35% by 2030. With a nearly unlimited source of power (salt water), nuclear fusion offers a sustainable solution to power communities. (PEW, CMU)
  • How AI Centers can Recycle and Give Back: Read more on AI Journ.

Public Opinion: New Generations with New Ideas

  • Public Opinion on Nuclear Energy in the U.S.: View 2025 Data.
  • The ITER Project: A "lab" for global collaboration between the US, EU, China, India, Japan, Korea, and Russia. It is the primary source for cutting-edge fusion tech. Visit ITER.org.
